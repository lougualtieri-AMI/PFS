You're looking at a planning sketch of your household's money over the years ahead — before and after retirement. Nothing you do here is saved or sent anywhere.

  1. See it work first. Pick a household from the “— sample households —” menu just below. The whole page fills in: where you stand today, what your savings can safely support each year, and what shifts under different choices.
  2. Read down the page. Where you are (today) → What it supports (a safe yearly spend) → What could happen (retire earlier, spend differently, move, sell the house, plan around taxes).
  3. Make it yours. Edit any number in place, or paste in your own data. Hover — or tap, on a phone — the underlined items and info marks for plain-language explanations.

On a phone? Turn on your browser's “Desktop site” so the wide tables fit on one screen.

Your privacy, plainly. The sample households are completely made up — invented so the app could be built and tested on realistic situations, never on real data. Anything you enter stays only in your browser, on your own computer: it is never sent anywhere, never saved to a server, and never seen by the developer or by the AI that helped build the app. Even the developer's own real finances stayed on his own machine the whole time — everything here was built and checked using only these made-up samples.
